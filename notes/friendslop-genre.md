# The Rise of Friendslop

"Friendslop" is an internet-originated term used to describe a video game genre characterized by low-budget, low-stakes, and often humorous co-op experiences designed primarily for playing with friends. These games typically feature simple objectives, goofy mechanics such as proximity chat or unusual physics engines, and are frequently indie titles. The core appeal lies in the shared experience and emergent gameplay that arises from playing with others, rather than intricate narratives or highly polished mechanics.

## Mount Your Friends (2014)

Developed and published by Stegersaurus Software Inc., *Mount Your Friends* was released in July 2014. This physics-based competitive climbing game challenges players to reach the highest point on a tower made of other players (and a goat), controlling each limb individually. Its intentionally clumsy mechanics lead to hilarious and often absurd situations, making it a quintessential "Friendslop" experience. While not a blockbuster, its unique blend of simple objectives and goofy physics carved out a niche and became a cult classic, celebrated for its couch co-op hilarity.

## Quiplash (2015)

Developed and published by Jackbox Games, Inc., *Quiplash* was first released in June 2015. This party game involves 3-8 players answering humorous prompts on their phones or tablets, with other players and an audience voting for the funniest or most clever responses. With no "correct" answers and a strong emphasis on player creativity and social interaction, *Quiplash* perfectly embodies the low-stakes, high-hilarity spirit of "Friendslop." Its success has spawned multiple sequels and variations, establishing it as a go-to title for group gatherings and online streams, primarily due to its accessible, inclusive, and endlessly repeatable gameplay that thrives on emergent humor.

## Ultimate Chicken Horse (2016)

Developed by Canadian studio Clever Endeavour Games, *Ultimate Chicken Horse* was released in March 2016. It is a multiplayer competitive platformer where players build the level as they play, placing traps and hazards to hinder their opponents while trying to navigate the level themselves. The game's unique blend of creative level-building and chaotic platforming perfectly captures the "Friendslop" spirit, as players create their own emergent fun through their shared creations and rivalries.

## Human: Fall Flat (2016)

Initially released in July 2016, *Human: Fall Flat* is a puzzle-platformer developed by Lithuanian studio No Brakes Games. Players control customizable characters in dreamscapes, solving puzzles with "weird body physics." Its success lies in its co-op mode, where the ragdoll physics and creative problem-solving lead to hilarious and unpredictable situations, making it a staple of the "Friendslop" genre.

## Genital Jousting (2016)

Developed by Free Lives and published by Devolver Digital, *Genital Jousting* entered Steam Early Access in November 2016. The game, which features players controlling disembodied penises to complete various tasks, is a prime example of the absurd and humorous gameplay that defines "Friendslop." Its focus on party modes and co-op challenges, combined with its intentionally provocative and silly premise, has made it a memorable and often-cited example of the genre.

## Among Us (2018)

Developed by the American game studio Innersloth, *Among Us* was initially released in 2018 with little mainstream attention. The game is an online multiplayer social deduction game where players work together on a spaceship, but one or more players are impostors trying to sabotage the ship and eliminate the crew. The game's success story is a testament to the power of community and timing. It wasn't until 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, that the game exploded in popularity, largely thanks to Twitch streamers and YouTubers. This surge in popularity led to over 100 million downloads and 1.5 million concurrent players by September 2020, making it a cultural phenomenon and a prime example of a "Friendslop" game.

## Heave Ho (2019)

Released in August 2019, *Heave Ho* is a party platformer from developer Le Cartel Studio and publisher Devolver Digital. Up to four players control characters with long, stretchy arms, and must work together to swing, climb, and throw each other to safety. The game's physics-based chaos and emphasis on teamwork (and the hilarious failures that come with it) make it a perfect "Friendslop" title.

## Phasmophobia (2020)

Released in September 2020, *Phasmophobia* is a 4-player online co-op psychological horror game developed by Kinetic Games. While its primary goal is to create a scary atmosphere, the game's reliance on co-op, proximity chat, and the often-hilarious emergent situations that arise from players' panicked reactions have made it a favorite among friends. It represents a sub-genre of "Friendslop" that blends horror with humor and social interaction.

## Lethal Company (2023)

*Lethal Company*, developed and published by a solo developer named Zeekerss, is a cooperative survival horror video game released in early access in October 2023. In the game, players work together to collect scrap from abandoned, industrialized moons to sell to "the Company." The game's success is attributed to its engaging social system, its appeal to social media, and its comparison to other popular horror games. It quickly gained popularity on Steam, topping the platform's Global Top Seller list and reaching over 100,000 concurrent players in November 2023. By January 2024, it was estimated to have sold more than 10 million copies.

## Content Warning (2024)

Released on April 1, 2024, *Content Warning* is a co-op horror game developed by a small team at Landfall Games. The game's premise is simple and hilarious: players film their friends in spooky situations to try and go viral on a fictional video-sharing platform. The game's success was immediate and massive, thanks in large part to a clever marketing strategy: it was offered for free for the first 24 hours, during which 6.2 million players claimed the game. Within two weeks of its release, the game sold 1 million copies, cementing its place as a "Friendslop" hit.

## Murky Divers (2024)

*Murky Divers*, from developer Embers, entered Early Access in June 2024. In this co-op horror game, players dive into the ocean to remove corpses from abandoned underwater labs. The dark humor of the premise, combined with the potential for chaotic physics-based interactions and proximity chat, firmly places it within the "Friendslop" genre.

## R.E.P.O. (2025)

*R.E.P.O.* (Retrieve, Extract, and Profit Operation) is a cooperative online survival horror game developed by Swedish studio Semiwork, which launched in early access on Steam in February 2025. In the game, players take on the role of interdimensional repo agents, tasked with retrieving valuable items from hazardous environments. The game has achieved considerable success, garnering an "Overwhelmingly Positive" rating on Steam and generating over $63 million in revenue within its first few months. Its viral appeal is attributed to features like physics-based loot, dynamic enemy encounters, and emergent gameplay, making it the latest in a line of successful "Friendslop" titles.

## PEAK (2025)

Developed in collaboration by Aggro Crab and Landfall Games, *PEAK* was released on June 16, 2025. This cooperative climbing video game challenges players, as lost nature scouts, to scale a mysterious island's central mountain to be rescued. Its challenging gameplay, where even minor errors can lead to failure, fosters the chaotic and humorous emergent situations characteristic of the "Friendslop" genre.
